## Signs of Sensitive Stomach

Your dog may have a sensitive stomach if they experience:

- **Frequent diarrhea** or loose stools
- **Vomiting** after meals
- **Excessive gas** or bloating
- **Loss of appetite**
- **Weight loss** despite normal eating
- **Skin issues** (often related to food allergies)

## Common Triggers

### Food Allergies

Most common allergens:
1. Beef (34% of cases)
2. Dairy (17%)
3. Chicken (15%)
4. Wheat (13%)
5. Soy (6%)

### Other Causes

- Sudden diet changes
- Eating too fast
- Low-quality ingredients
- Artificial additives
- Stress or anxiety

## Elimination Diet Guide

If you suspect food allergies, try an elimination diet:

### Week 1-2: Transition
- Gradually introduce new food over 7-10 days
- No treats, table scraps, or flavored medications

### Week 3-8: Monitor
- Feed ONLY the new food
- Track symptoms daily
- Note any improvements

### Week 9+: Reintroduce
- If symptoms improve, slowly reintroduce old ingredients one at a time
- Watch for symptom recurrence

## Ingredients to Look For

### Good for Sensitive Stomachs

- ✅ **Easily digestible proteins:** Salmon, lamb, venison
- ✅ **Prebiotics:** Chicory root, beet pulp
- ✅ **Probiotics:** Live active cultures
- ✅ **Fiber:** Pumpkin, sweet potato
- ✅ **Omega-3s:** Fish oil for inflammation

### Avoid if Possible

- ❌ **Artificial colors and flavors**
- ❌ **By-products and fillers**
- ❌ **Common allergens** (if your dog is sensitive)
- ❌ **Excessive fat**

## FAQ

### How long does it take to see improvement?

Most dogs show improvement within 2-4 weeks of switching to a sensitive stomach formula.

### Should I feed wet or dry food?

Both can work. Wet food may be easier to digest, but dry food helps with dental health. Some owners mix both.

### Can I give my dog probiotics?

Yes! Probiotics can help restore gut health. Look for dog-specific supplements with multiple strains.
