## Is Your Dog Overweight?

Signs your dog may need to lose weight:

- **Ribs not easily felt** when you run your hands along their sides
- **No visible waist** when viewed from above
- **Abdominal tuck absent** when viewed from the side
- **Difficulty breathing** or exercising
- **Reluctance to exercise** or play

## Why Weight Matters

Obesity affects 60% of dogs and can lead to:

- Diabetes
- Arthritis and joint problems
- Heart disease
- Reduced lifespan (up to 2.5 years shorter!)
- Decreased quality of life

## Weight Loss Tips

### Portion Control

- Measure food with a proper measuring cup
- Follow feeding guidelines based on TARGET weight, not current weight
- Reduce treats to less than 10% of daily calories

### Exercise

- Start with short, frequent walks
- Gradually increase duration
- Try swimming for low-impact exercise
- Play interactive games (fetch, tug)

### Feeding Schedule

- **2-3 smaller meals** per day instead of 1 large meal
- **Slow feeder bowls** to prevent rapid eating
- **Puzzle toys** for mental stimulation and slower eating

## How Much Weight Should Your Dog Lose?

Safe weight loss: **1-2% of body weight per week**

## FAQ

### How long does it take for a dog to lose weight?

Most dogs can safely lose weight in 2-6 months, depending on how much they need to lose.

### Can I just feed my dog less of their current food?

Not recommended. Weight loss formulas have fewer calories but maintain essential nutrients. Simply feeding less may cause nutrient deficiencies.

### Should I exercise my dog more while dieting?

Yes, but start gradually. Overweight dogs may have joint issues. Consult your vet for an appropriate exercise plan.
