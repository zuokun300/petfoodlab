## Buyer's Guide {#buyers-guide}

### What to Look for in Dog Food {#what-to-look-for}

#### 1. Protein Source
Look for **named meat** (chicken, beef, lamb) as the first ingredient, not "meat by-products" or "meat meal".

#### 2. AAFCO Statement
Ensures the food meets **complete nutrition standards** for your dog's life stage.

#### 3. Life Stage Appropriateness
- **Puppy**: Higher protein and calories for growth
- **Adult**: Balanced nutrition for maintenance
- **Senior**: Lower calories, joint support

#### 4. Special Dietary Needs
- **Grain-free**: For dogs with grain allergies
- **Limited ingredient**: For sensitive stomachs
- **Weight management**: For overweight dogs

### Ingredients to Avoid {#ingredients-to-avoid}

❌ **Artificial colors** (Red 40, Yellow 5, Blue 2)
❌ **Artificial preservatives** (BHA, BHT, ethoxyquin)
❌ **Meat by-products** (unnamed animal parts)
❌ **Fillers** (corn syrup, sugar, cellulose)

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is grain-free better for dogs? {#is-grain-free-better}

**Not necessarily.** Unless your dog has a diagnosed grain allergy (which is rare), grains can be a healthy source of carbohydrates, fiber, and nutrients. Grain-free diets have been linked to DCM (dilated cardiomyopathy) in some cases.

### How much should I feed my dog?

Follow the **feeding guide on the package**, adjusted for:
- Your dog's weight
- Activity level
- Age
- Body condition score

Always consult your vet for personalized recommendations.

### Can I switch dog food brands?

**Yes, but transition gradually** over 7-10 days:
- Days 1-3: 75% old food, 25% new food
- Days 4-6: 50% old food, 50% new food
- Days 7-9: 25% old food, 75% new food
- Day 10+: 100% new food

This prevents digestive upset.

### How do I know if my dog likes the food?

Signs your dog approves:
- ✅ Eats eagerly at mealtime
- ✅ Finishes meals consistently
- ✅ Good energy levels
- ✅ Healthy coat and skin
- ✅ Normal stool quality
