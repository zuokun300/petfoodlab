## Why Senior Dogs Need Special Food

As dogs age (typically 7+ years), their nutritional needs change:

- **Lower calories** to prevent weight gain
- **Higher fiber** for digestive health
- **Joint support** (glucosamine, chondroitin)
- **Antioxidants** for immune support
- **Easy-to-digest protein** for aging kidneys

## Senior Dog Health Concerns

### Weight Management

Older dogs are less active and prone to weight gain. Look for:
- Lower calorie formulas
- L-carnitine for metabolism
- Higher fiber for satiety

### Joint Health

Arthritis affects 20% of adult dogs. Look for:
- Glucosamine
- Chondroitin
- Omega-3 fatty acids

### Digestive Health

Aging affects digestion. Look for:
- Prebiotics and probiotics
- Easy-to-digest proteins
- Moderate fiber content

## FAQ

### At what age is a dog considered senior?

It depends on breed size. Small dogs become seniors at 10-12 years, while large breeds may be seniors at 6-7 years.

### Should senior dogs eat less protein?

Not necessarily. Senior dogs need high-quality, easily digestible protein. Only reduce protein if kidney disease is present.

### How often should senior dogs eat?

Most senior dogs do well with 2 meals per day. Some may benefit from smaller, more frequent meals if they have digestive issues.
